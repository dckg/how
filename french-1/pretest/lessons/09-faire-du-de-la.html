❤️ Section 1 — Aimer (Liking)

Verbs of liking use the definite article. The article stays the same in negation.

Sujet✅ Affirmatif❌ Négatif
jeJ'aime le ski.I like skiing.Je n'aime pas le ski.I don't like skiing.
tuTu aimes le yoga.You like yoga.Tu n'aimes pas le yoga.You don't like yoga.
il / elleIl aime la danse.He likes dance.Il n'aime pas la danse.He doesn't like dance.
nousNous aimons l'escrime.We like fencing.Nous n'aimons pas l'escrime.We don't like fencing.
vousVous aimez les films.You like films.Vous n'aimez pas les films.You don't like films.

💪 Section 2 — Faire (Doing)

Uses du, de la, de l', des (doing some of an activity). In negation, these become de.

Sujet✅ Affirmatif❌ Négatif
jeJe fais du ski.I do (some) skiing.Je ne fais pas de ski.I don't do (any) skiing.
tuTu fais du yoga.You do (some) yoga.Tu ne fais pas de yoga.You don't do (any) yoga.
il / elleIl fait de la danse.He does (some) dance.Il ne fait pas de danse.He doesn't do (any) dance.
nousNous faisons de l'escrime.We do (some) fencing.Nous ne faisons pas d'escrime.We don't do (any) fencing.
vousVous faites des arts martiaux.You do (some) martial arts.Vous ne faites pas d'arts martiaux.You don't do (any) martial arts.

🚫 Section 3 — Negation

Je n'aime pas le yoga.I don't like yoga.

Category stays → article unchanged

Je ne fais pas de yoga.I don't do (any) yoga.

Zero participation → du/de la/des become de

After negation, du/de la/des → de (or d' before a vowel).

⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ Don't say✅ Say insteadWhy
*J'aime du yoga. J'aime le yoga. Liking = category → le/la/les
*Je fais le ski. Je fais du ski. Doing = episodes → du/de la/des
*Je ne fais pas du vélo. Je ne fais pas de vélo. Negation of faire → bare de
*Je n'aime pas de boxe. Je n'aime pas la boxe. Negation of liking → article unchanged
*Je aime le ski. J'aime le ski. Elision: je + vowel → j'
💡 The #1 rule: Ask yourself — am I expressing a feeling about the activity (category → le/la), or am I doing the activity (episodes → du/de la)? The verb tells you which mental image to use.

Le concept — What this tab is for

Understand the conceptual difference between aimer (liking a category) and faire (doing an activity). Read through the explanations, then move on to Les formes to see the grammar in action.

💡

Le concept

One question

Why does:  J'aime le yoga  become  Je fais du yoga ?

The answer (in one sentence)

Because aimer talks about a category, and faire talks about participating in it.

1️⃣  Liking = The Activity as a Whole

When you use aimer / adorer / détester, you are talking about the activity in general.
You are naming the category.

J'aime le yoga.I like yoga.  — I like yoga (as a type of activity).

When you talk about a whole category in French, you use:

le  /  la  /  l'  /  les

Think:  🗂️ The idea of yoga.

2️⃣  Doing = Taking Part in It

When you use faire, you are talking about actually doing the activity.
You are participating in instances of it.

Je fais du yoga.I do (some) yoga.  — I do (some) yoga.

When you talk about participating in an activity, French adds de to the article:

Category Doing
le yogadu yoga
la dansede la danse
l'escrimede l'escrime
les arts martiauxdes arts martiaux

Think:  📅 Actual sessions of yoga.

3️⃣  What Happens in Negation?

Now look at the difference carefully.

Liking (category stays)

Je n'aime pas le yoga.I don't like yoga.

You still mean the category. So the article does not change.

Doing (zero participation)

Je ne fais pas de yoga.I don't do (any) yoga.

You are saying there are zero sessions. So French removes the article and keeps only:

de  /  d'

The Whole System in 20 Seconds

❤️ Aimer → category → le / la / les
💪 Faire → participation → du / de la / des
🚫 Ne pas faire → zero participation → de

If you remember only one thing

Liking = the activity as a whole.  Doing = taking part in it.
Everything else follows from that.
