One noun, four roles

Same word — théâtre. The verb sets the construction. Watch de le and à le fuse.

J’aime le théâtre
❤️theatre as what I like (a domain)
Je fais de ledu théâtre
💪theatre as what I do (a practice)
Je vais à leau théâtre
🚶theatre as where I go (a destination)
Je suis à leau théâtre
📍theatre as where I am (a location)
Je viens de ledu théâtre
↩️theatre as where I come from (an origin)
Je suis de ledu théâtre
📍theatre as where I am from (an origin)

The verb determines the construction — which preposition (if any) and which article type. de + le → du and à + le → au are obligatory contractions, not choices. The noun’s role — domain, activity, destination, location, origin — is set by the verb.
